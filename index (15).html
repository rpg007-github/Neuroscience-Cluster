Our Mission

The UWF Neuroscience Research Cluster unites investigators across biology, computational science, public health, and psychology to address the defining challenges in brain science. We share data, trainees, and ideas across departmental boundaries because the brain does not respect them.

▲

Discovery

Basic and translational research in neurodegeneration, cognition, and neural computation

▲

Training

NIH-funded undergraduate and graduate mentorship through the NeuroNauts Scholars Program

▲

Translation

Community engagement, clinical partnerships, and real-world impact in Northwest Florida

Research

Converging on the Brain
from Every Direction

Our investigators span four domains that together offer a comprehensive lens on how the nervous system works, fails, adapts, and heals.

01

Neurodegeneration & Alzheimer's Disease

Biomarker discovery via phage display, calpain proteolysis, tau biology, and oxidative stress pathways. Spatial epidemiology of AD mortality clustering. Clinical biomarker development for early detection.

Tau Biology Phage Display Biomarkers Calpain
02

Cognitive & Computational Neuroscience

Neural signal decoding, prefrontal cortex dynamics, working memory systems, EEG-based biomarkers for cognitive impairment, machine learning models of brain function, and flash visual evoked potentials.

EEG Working Memory Neural Decoding Machine Learning
03

Cellular & Developmental Neuroscience

Cellular mechanisms underlying neural circuit formation, synaptogenesis, and neuroplasticity. Parkinson's disease models and exercise-induced neuroprotection. Neural regeneration and neurogenesis research.

Neuroplasticity Parkinson's Neurogenesis Synaptogenesis
04

Community Neuroscience & Health Disparities

Stroke prevention, brain health equity across underserved communities in Northwest Florida, community-based participatory research, and population-level surveillance of neurological conditions.

Stroke Health Equity Disparities Community Health
The Team

Founded on
Shared Curiosity

Our cluster brings together leading investigators whose work, combined, spans the full arc of neuroscience — from molecule to community.

RG
Cluster Director • Founding Investigator

Rodney Guttmann, Ph.D.

Professor & Interim Chair, Dept. of Biology • HMCSE

Over 30 years of NIH-funded research in Alzheimer's disease biomarker discovery, calpain proteolysis, tau biology, and phage display technology. Holds two U.S. patents and has mentored more than 100 undergraduate researchers. PI of the NIH R25 NeuroNauts Scholars Program (NINDS, $555,237).

Alzheimer's Disease Phage Display Tau Biology NIH Funded
SP
Co-Founder • Computational Lead

Shusen Pu, Ph.D.

Department of Mathematics & Statistics • HMCSE

Computational neuroscientist specializing in prefrontal cortex dynamics, neural signal decoding, and the mathematics of working memory. Applies machine learning and stochastic modeling to understand how neural populations encode and transform information in the absence of task execution.

Computational Neuroscience Neural Decoding PFC Dynamics Machine Learning
PC
Co-Founder • Cellular Neuroscience

Peter Cavnar, Ph.D.

Department of Biology • HMCSE

Cellular and developmental neuroscientist investigating the molecular mechanisms of neural circuit formation and synaptic function. Research bridges fundamental cell biology with neural system behavior, providing essential mechanistic grounding for translational neuroscience initiatives within the cluster.

Cellular Neuroscience Synaptogenesis Neural Circuits Neuroplasticity
DT
Co-Founder • Public Health Neuroscience

Daudet Ilunga Tshiswaka, Ph.D.

Department of Public Health • Halsa School of Health

Public health researcher focused on brain health disparities, stroke prevention, and community-based health interventions in underserved populations across Northwest Florida. Expertise in participatory research, health surveillance, and the social determinants of neurological outcomes.

Stroke Health Disparities Community Research Brain Health Equity
